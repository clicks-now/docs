# Clicks — Mods Overview

*A quick read for people who want to help make this run.*

---

## What we're building

Clicks is a city-wide partner meetup series for the Shopify ecosystem. Same format, every city. 2 hours, hard out. No production budget, no fluff.

We're starting in Toronto and building the infrastructure to take it to every major Shopify city — with a small team of people who care about the community.

---

## What mods do

Mods are the people who keep things running between events. The role is light by design — this is a side project for all of us.

**Reviewing host requests**
When someone wants to run Clicks in their city, they submit a request. Mods review it, ask any questions, and approve or pass. Most requests take 10 minutes to assess.

**Approving content**
The [clicks-now GitHub org](https://github.com/clicks-now) is the open infrastructure behind the community — event records, the handbook, dispatch posts. Mods review PRs and merge what belongs.

**Supporting hosts**
Once an event is approved, the host gets a marketing pack and playbook. Mods are the point of contact if they get stuck or need something.

**Keeping the Discord healthy**
Light moderation. Answer questions. Welcome new people. Flag anything that needs attention.

That's mostly it. We're not building a company here.

---

## What we're not asking for

- A set number of hours per week
- You to attend every event
- You to be a Shopify expert
- Any financial commitment

---

## How it works in practice

Everything runs through three tools:

- **Discord** — day-to-day conversation, host support, community
- **Asana** — tracking host requests and event progress
- **GitHub** — event records, content, handbook (you don't need to know Git deeply to contribute)

When a host request comes in, it hits our Asana board and Discord simultaneously. Whoever's available picks it up.

---

## What we're looking for in mods

- You're connected to the Shopify ecosystem in some way
- You care about the community being good, not just big
- You're direct and reliable — if you say you'll handle something, you handle it
- You're okay with things being a bit unfinished while we figure it out
